About the Conference:

     Welcome to the Mediterranean Smart Cities Conference taking place in the Mediterranean cities of Martil and Tetuan, Morocco, where innovation meets urban evolution in the cradle of history. Join us for a journey through the vibrant and dynamic landscape of smart cities, where tradition meets technology, and ancient streets converge with futuristic possibilities.

     This conference will bring together thought leaders, policymakers, urban planners, technology experts, and PhD Students to explore the future of smart cities through different activities including keynote talks, technical sessions, panels, workshops, and tutorials.

Key Topics

  • Topics of the conference include but are not limited to:
  • Smart Cities Technologies and Applications: Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, Big Data, 5G, 6G, IoT, Smart Grid, Smart Education, Smart Agriculture, Smart Health, Smart Economy, Smart Mobility, Cyber Security, Smart Building and Demotic Automation, Smart Electrical / Electronic devices, Microelectronic design, Smart Services, Smart Manufacturing, etc.
  • Digital Transformation: Explore the role of technology in reshaping urban landscapes and enhancing citizen experiences.
  • Sustainable Urban Planning: Delve into sustainable development practices and their impact on the environment and quality of life.
  • Community Engagement: Learn how involving citizens in decision-making processes can lead to more inclusive and vibrant cities.
  • Data Analytics for Smart Cities: Harness the power of data to optimize urban services, from transportation to energy management.
  • Innovation in Infrastructure: Discover the latest trends in smart infrastructure design and implementation.
  • Resilience and Climate Action: Address the challenges of climate change and strategies for building resilient cities.
  • Digital Inclusion: Ensure that the benefits of smart cities are accessible to all, bridging the digital divide.

Instructions to Authors

Prospective authors are invited to submit papers using the Microsoft Conference Management Toolkit. The authors will need to register their email with CMT. Here is a link how to create a CMT account.
All papers must adhere to the following formatting:
  • Papers should be original and not currently under review at another venue.
  • Conference language is English, no exceptions.
  • Papers accepted only in PDF format, unlocked.
  • Number of pages per paper is 4 to 6.
  • Basic guidelines for preparing papers for conference proceedings are available in TEMPLATE (.DOCX) (based on IEEE® Template).
    • Paper Template Submit Your Paper Registration
  • Publication options:
    - Accepted papers will be submitted for inclusion into IEEE Xplore subject to meeting IEEE Xplore’s scope and quality requirements.


    - Authors of selected papers will be invited to submit an extended version of their manuscripts for journal publication in a special issues/sections of relevant and renowned journals (Scopus indexed Journals).

    - Selected of submitted papers may be recommended for publication in Special Issues titled: "Selected Problems Arising in the Development of Sustainable Transport Systems" of Sustainability journal and "Methods and Technologies to Support Energy Management Decision Making in the Transport Sector" of Energies journal with special discount.

    - Authors of selected papers will be invited to submit an extended version of their manuscripts to the : "Scalable Computing: Practice and Experience Journal".
